### 1. **Mechanics of Transformer Processing**
- **Layered Architecture**: Input text is processed through multiple layers. Each layer performs two main operations:
  - **Self-Attention**: Allows each token to attend to all previous tokens, capturing contextual relationships.
  - **Feedforward Network (FFN)**: Transforms the attended representation into a new one.
- **Mixture-of-Experts (MoE)**: In MoE variants, the FFN is replaced by a gating mechanism that dynamically selects a subset of expert modules for each token. This allows the model to specialize:
  - **Gating Function**: Scores all available experts based on the current token representation.
  - **Routing**: Only the top-k experts (e.g., top 2) are activated per token.
  - **Weighted Combination**: Outputs from selected experts are combined and passed forward.
- **Progressive Refinement**: This process repeats across layers, refining the representation until the final layer projects it to vocabulary logits for next-token prediction.
