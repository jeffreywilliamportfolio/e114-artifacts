Photosynthesis is the process by which plants, algae, and some bacteria convert sunlight into energy. It consists of two main stages: the **light reactions** and the **Calvin cycle**.

### Light Reactions
The light reactions occur in the chloroplasts, specifically in the thyl membranes. They are divided into two parts:
1. **Light absorption**: The chloroplasts absorb light energy from the sun.
2. **Light conversion**: The chloroplasts convert light energy into chemical energy.

### Calvin Cycle
The Calvin cycle is the process by which plants, algae, and some bacteria convert chemical energy into glucose. It consists of three steps:
1. **Light absorption**: The chloroplasts absorb light energy from the sun.
2. **Light conversion**: The chloroplasts convert light energy into chemical energy.
3. **Light conversion**: The chloroplasts convert light energy into glucose.
