Photosynthesis is the biological process by which plants, algae, and certain bacteria convert light energy into chemical energy stored in glucose. This process occurs primarily in the **chloroplasts** of plant cells and can be divided into two main stages: the **Light-Dependent Reactions** and the **Calvin Cycle** (Light-Independent Reactions).

Here is a step-by-step explanation of how it works:

### 1. The Light-Dependent Reactions
**Location:** Thylakoid membranes of the chloroplast.
**Goal:** Convert solar energy into chemical energy carriers (ATP and NADPH) and release oxygen.

1.  **Absorption of Light:** Chlorophyll and other pigments in the photosystems (Photosystem II and Photosystem I) absorb photons (light energy).
2.  **Water Splitting (Photolysis):** In Photosystem II, the absorbed energy excites electrons. To replace these lost electrons, water molecules ($H_2O$) are split. This releases:
    *   **Oxygen ($O_2$):** Released as a byproduct into the atmosphere.
    *   **Protons ($H^+$):** Released into the thylakoid space.
    *   **Electrons ($e^-$):** Passed down an electron transport chain (ETC).
3.  **Electron Transport Chain:** As excited electrons move through the ETC, their energy is used to pump protons ($H^+$) from the stroma into the thylakoid space, creating a high concentration gradient.
4.  **ATP Synthesis:** The protons flow back into the stroma through an enzyme called **ATP Synthase**. This flow drives the production of **ATP** (Adenosine Triphosphate) from ADP and inorganic phosphate.
5.  **NADPH Formation:** The electrons eventually reach Photosystem I, where they are re-energized by light and transferred to the carrier molecule **NADP+**, reducing it to **NADPH**.

**Output of Light Reactions:** ATP, NADPH, and $O_2$ (waste product).

---

### 2. The Calvin Cycle (Light-Independent Reactions)
**Location:** Stroma of the chloroplast (the fluid surrounding the thylakoids).
**Goal:** Use the ATP and NADPH from the light reactions to fix carbon dioxide into glucose.

1.  **Carbon Fixation:** An enzyme called **RuBisCO** attaches a carbon atom from carbon dioxide ($CO_2$) to a 5-carbon sugar called RuBP (Ribulose bisphosphate). This creates an unstable 6-carbon compound that immediately splits into two 3-carbon molecules (3-PGA).
2.  **Reduction:** The 3-PGA molecules are converted into G3P (Glyceraldehyde-3-phosphate), a simple sugar. This step requires energy from **ATP** and electrons from **NADPH** (produced in the light reactions).
    *   *Note:* For every 3 molecules of $CO_2$ fixed, one molecule of G3P exits the cycle to be used for glucose synthesis.
3.  **Regeneration:** The remaining G3P molecules are rearranged using more **ATP** to regenerate the original RuBP acceptor molecule, allowing the cycle to continue.

**Output of Calvin Cycle:** G3P (which is used to build glucose, starch, and cellulose).

---

### Summary Equation
The overall chemical equation for photosynthesis summarizes these two stages:

$$6CO_2 + 6H_2O + \text{Light Energy} \rightarrow C_6H_{12}O_6 (\text{Glucose}) + 6O_2$$

### Key Takeaways
*   **Interdependence:** The light reactions provide the energy (ATP) and reducing power (NADPH) needed for the Calvin cycle. The Calvin cycle regenerates the ADP and NADP+ needed for the light reactions to continue.
*   **Energy Transformation:** Light energy $\rightarrow$ Chemical energy (ATP/NADPH) $\rightarrow$ Stable chemical energy (Glucose).
*   **Oxygen Source:** The oxygen released into our atmosphere comes specifically from the splitting of water during the light reactions, not from the carbon dioxide.
