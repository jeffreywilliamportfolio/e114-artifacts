### Mechanical Report: CPU Processing Under Heavy Load

When a CPU operates under heavy load, the architecture shifts from idle power-saving states (C-states) and low-frequency scaling to maximum voltage, frequency, and parallel throughput. The process is a continuous, high-speed loop of data movement and logical execution, governed by the system clock and physical electrical properties of the silicon.

Here is the mechanical breakdown of the events occurring within the processor die during a sustained heavy load scenario (e.g., 100% utilization across all cores).

#### 1. Frequency and Voltage Scaling (DVFS)
The first mechanical adjustment occurs at the clock generation and power delivery level.
*   **Voltage Increase:** The power management unit (PMU) increases the voltage supplied to the cores (Vcore). In modern Intel (Turbo Boost) or AMD (Precision Boost) architectures, voltage may rise from a baseline of ~0.8V to ~1.3V+ to ensure electrons have enough energy to traverse the transistor channels quickly without timing errors.
