Comparing monotheistic traditions (such as Judaism, Christianity, and Islam) with non-theistic traditions (primarily Buddhism, Jainism, and certain schools of thought within Hinduism like Advaita Vedanta) reveals distinct differences in how they conceptualize the ultimate reality, the role of a creator, and the path to spiritual liberation.

Here is a comparison across theology and practice:

### 1. Theological Foundations

#### **Monotheistic Traditions**
*   **Core Belief:** These traditions posit the existence of a single, personal God (often referred to as Allah, Yahweh, or God). This deity is usually defined as omnipotent, omniscient, and omnibenevolent.
*   **Creator vs. Creation:** The God is distinct from the universe (transcendent). The universe is created *ex nihilo* (from nothing) by this divine will.
*   **Soul and Self:** There is usually a belief in an eternal soul or self that survives the body and is judged by the Creator.
*   **Purpose:** History and life are often viewed as linear, moving toward a specific goal (e.g., the End of History, the Kingdom of God, or the Day of Judgment).

#### **Non-Theistic Traditions**
*   **Core Belief:** These traditions do not necessarily deny God's existence, but they often suspend the question or focus on the mechanics of the universe rather than a creator.
    *   **Buddhism:** Explicitly agnostic or non-theistic regarding a creator God. It focuses on the law of *Karma* and *Dependent Origination* (all things arise from causes and conditions).
    *   **Jainism:** Believes in an eternal universe with souls (*Jiva*) and matter (*Ajiva*), governed by natural laws, without a specific creator deity.
    *   **Advaita Vedanta (Hinduism):** While it acknowledges a personal God (*Ishvara*), it posits that the ultimate reality is *Brahman* (an impersonal, non-dual consciousness). The personal God is a lower level of reality for those who need a deity to worship.
*   **Creator vs. Creation:** The universe is often viewed as eternal or cyclical. It operates according to natural laws rather than a divine decree.
*   **Soul and Self:** In Buddhism, the concept of *Anatta* (no-self) is central. There is no permanent soul; continuity is maintained through the stream of consciousness and karma.

### 2. The Path to Liberation (Soteriology)

#### **Monotheistic Traditions**
*   **The Goal:** Salvation is often achieved through **faith** and **grace**. The goal is to be reconciled with the Creator, often escaping the cycle of suffering or death.
*   **The Method:**
    *   **Devotion:** Prayer, worship, and adherence to divine laws (Dharma/Sharia).
    *   **Judgment:** The path is often mediated by the will of the God. If one aligns with the divine will, they are saved; if not, they face punishment.
    *   **Authority:** Truth is revealed through scripture and the authority of the prophet or priestly class.

#### **Non-Theistic Traditions**
*   **The Goal:** Liberation (*Moksha* or *Nirvana*) is the cessation of suffering (*Dukkha*) and the breaking of the cycle of rebirth. It is often described as a state of being rather than a relationship with a deity.
*   **The Method:**
    *   **Empirical Practice:** The path is often described as a "science of the mind." It relies on observation, meditation, and logic rather than faith alone.
    *   **Self-Reliance:** The practitioner must work out their own liberation. As the Buddha famously said, "Be a lamp unto yourself."
    *   **Discipline:** The focus is on ethical conduct, concentration, and wisdom (the Noble Eightfold Path).

### 3. Comparison of Practice

| Feature | Monotheistic Traditions | Non-Theistic Traditions |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Ritual Focus** | **Communal Worship:** Emphasis on congregational prayer, sacraments, and feasts to honor the Creator. | **Individual Meditation:** Emphasis on solitary practice (meditation, yoga, mindfulness) to alter consciousness. |
| **View of Suffering** | **External Cause:** Suffering is often a result of sin, the fall of man, or the will of God. | **Internal Cause:** Suffering is a result of ignorance, attachment, and the cycle of Karma. |
| **Authority** | **Revelation:** Truth is found in holy texts (Bible, Quran
