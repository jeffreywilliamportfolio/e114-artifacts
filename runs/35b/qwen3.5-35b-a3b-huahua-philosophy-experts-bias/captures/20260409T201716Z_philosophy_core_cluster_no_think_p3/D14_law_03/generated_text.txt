**Statutory Interpretation** vs. **Precedent**

In modern legal reasoning, the distinction between statutory interpretation and precedent is crucial. While statutory interpretation focuses on the literal meaning of the law, precedent relies on the historical context and the application of past rulings.

### **Statutory Interpretation**
Statutory interpretation involves the analysis of the text of the law itself. It is the process of determining the meaning of the law based on its written form. This method is often used to determine the exact meaning of the law, which is the basis for the legal interpretation.

### **Precedent**
Precedent, on the other hand, is the historical context and the application of past rulings. It is the process of determining the meaning of the law based on the past rulings. This method is often used to determine the exact meaning of the law, which is the basis for the legal interpretation.

### **Comparison**
Statutory interpretation and precedent are both methods of legal reasoning. They are both used to determine the exact meaning of the law, which is the basis for the legal interpretation.

### **Conclusion**
Statutory interpretation and precedent are both methods of legal reasoning. They are both used to determine the exact meaning of the law, which is the basis for the legal interpretation.
