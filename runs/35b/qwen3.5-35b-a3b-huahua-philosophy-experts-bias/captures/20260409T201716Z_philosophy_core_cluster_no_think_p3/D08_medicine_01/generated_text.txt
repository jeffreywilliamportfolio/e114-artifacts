Sepsis is a systemic response to infection, where the body's immune system tries to fight the infection but overreacts. It is medically dangerous because it can cause the body to go into a state of extreme inflammation, leading to organ dysfunction and death.

**How it develops:**
1. **Infection:** A bacteria or virus enters the body.
2. **Immune Response:** The immune system tries to fight the infection.
3. **Overreaction:** The immune system overreacts, causing inflammation.

**Why it is medically dangerous:**
1. **Organ Dysfunction:** The inflammation causes organs to dysfunction.
2. **Death:** The inflammation leads to death.

**Example:**
- A bacteria enters the body.
- The immune system tries to fight the infection.
- The immune system overreacts, causing inflammation.
- The inflammation causes organs to dysfunction.
- The inflammation leads to death.
