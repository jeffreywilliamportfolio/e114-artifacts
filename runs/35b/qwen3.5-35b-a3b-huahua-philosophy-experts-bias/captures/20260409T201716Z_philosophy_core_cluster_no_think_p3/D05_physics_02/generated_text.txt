Albert Einstein was a pivotal figure in the development of modern physics, particularly in the areas of relativity, quantum theory, and the foundational principles of physics. His contributions were significant because he introduced revolutionary concepts that reshaped the understanding of the universe.

### Einstein's Contributions to Relativity

Einstein's work on **relativity** was groundbreaking. He introduced the concept of **time and space** as a unified dimension, where time and space are not absolute but relative to the observer. This concept was a significant departure from the classical Newtonian view of the universe, where time and space were considered as a fixed, absolute background. Einstein's theory of **relativity** was a significant departure from the classical Newtonian view of the universe, where time and space were considered as a fixed, absolute background.
