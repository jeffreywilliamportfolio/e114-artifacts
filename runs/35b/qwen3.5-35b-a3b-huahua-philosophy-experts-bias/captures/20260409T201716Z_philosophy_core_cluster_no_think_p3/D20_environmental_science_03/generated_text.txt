**Ecology** and **Environmental Chemistry** are distinct disciplines that approach environmental risk assessment differently, focusing on different aspects of the natural system.

### **Ecology**
Ecology focuses on the **biological and systemic interactions** within a natural environment. It approaches risk assessment by evaluating the impact of human-made or natural disturbances on the ecosystem's structure and function.

*   **Approance:** It assesses risk by measuring the **loss of biodiversity, structural complexity, and functional stability**.
*   **Methodology:** It relies on **biological indicators** (e.g., species diversity, population density, trophic levels) to determine the health of the ecosystem.
*   **Goal:** It seeks to identify the **thresholds** where the ecosystem's resilience is compromised.

### **Environmental Chemistry**
Environmental Chemistry focuses on the **physical and chemical composition** of the environment. It approaches risk assessment by analyzing the molecular and chemical interactions within the natural system.

*   **Approance:** It assesses risk by measuring the **impact of chemical pollutants on the physical system**.
*   **Methodology:** It relies on **chemical indicators** (e.g., concentration, toxicity, molecular structure) to determine the health of the environment.
*   **Goal:** It seeks to identify the **thresholds** where the physical system's integrity is compromised.

### **Comparison**
While **Ecology** focuses on the **biological and systemic interactions**, **Environmental Chemistry** focuses on the **physical and chemical composition**.

In summary:
*   **Ecology** assesses risk by measuring the **loss of biodiversity**.
*   **Environmental Chemistry** assesses risk by measuring the **impact of chemical pollutants**.
