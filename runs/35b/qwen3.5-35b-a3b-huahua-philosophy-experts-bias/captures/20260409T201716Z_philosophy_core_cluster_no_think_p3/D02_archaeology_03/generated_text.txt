**Radiocarbon dating** and **stratigraphy** are complementary tools in archaeological interpretation, serving distinct but interconnected roles.

### 1. **Radiocarbon Dating**
Radiocarbon dating is a scientific method used to determine the age of organic materials (such as wood, charcoal, or bone) based on the decay of the carbon-14 isototope. It provides a **quantitative** framework for time, allowing archaeologists to establish a chronological depth for artifacts beyond the visual inspection of layers.

### 2. **Stratigraphy**
Stratigraphy is a visual and conceptual method used to analyze the spatial arrangement of archaeological deposits. It refers to the "digging" of layers in the ground, where the archaeologist observes the **vertical** sequence of artifacts. It provides a **qualitative** framework for time, allowing archaeologists to establish a chronological depth for artifacts beyond the visual inspection of layers.

### 3. **Comparison**
While radiocarbon dating offers a precise numerical age, stratigraphy offers a visual and conceptual method used to analyze the spatial arrangement of archaeological deposits. It refers to the "digging" of layers in the ground, where the archaeologist observes the **vertical** sequence of artifacts. It provides a **qualitative** framework for time, allowing archaeologists to establish a chronological depth for artifacts beyond the visual inspection of layers.

### 4. **Conclusion**
Ultimately, radiocarbon dating and stratigraphy are complementary tools in archaeological interpretation, serving distinct but interconnected roles.
