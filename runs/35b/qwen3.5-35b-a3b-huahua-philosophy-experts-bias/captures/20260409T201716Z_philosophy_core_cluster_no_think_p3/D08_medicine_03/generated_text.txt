**Antibiotics** and **Vaccines** are distinct tools in the arsenal of disease management, but they operate in different domains: one targets the *internal* biological environment, while the other targets the *external* environmental threat.

Here is a comparison of how they prevent or manage disease:

### 1. The Mechan of Action
*   **Antibiotics** are designed to **kill bacteria** (or fungi) within the body. They are chemical agents that disrupt bacterial cell walls or metabolic processes.
    *   **Prevention:** They are used to *prevent* the growth of bacteria that already exist in the body.
    *   **Management:** They are used to *manage* the infection that has already occurred.
*   **Vaccines** are designed to **train the immune system** to recognize a specific threat. They are biological agents that "program" the body's immune system to attack a specific pathogen.
    *   **Prevention:** They are used to *prevent* the pathogen from entering the body.
    *   **Management:** They are used to *manage* the disease that has already occurred.
