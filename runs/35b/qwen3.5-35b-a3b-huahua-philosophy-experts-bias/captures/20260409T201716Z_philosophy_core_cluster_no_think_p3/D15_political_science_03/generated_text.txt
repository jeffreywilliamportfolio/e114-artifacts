**Nationalism** and **Liberalism** are two distinct organizing ideas in modern political systems, often competing or complementary to each other. Here is a comparison:

### **Nationalism**
- **Focuses on identity and heritage**: It emphasizes the cultural, ethnic, or historical identity of a nation, often rooted in shared traditions, language, and ancestry.
- **Proms unity and cohesion**: It seeks to strengthen the collective identity of a nation, fostering unity and cohesion among its citizens.
- **Priorizes loyalty and loyalty**: It prioritizes loyalty to the nation, often emphasizing the duty of citizens to the nation's heritage and traditions.
- **Emphases exclusivity**: It emphasizes exclusivity, often prioritizing the identity of a nation over the identity of other nations.

### **Liberalism**
- **Focuses on individual rights**: It emphasizes the rights of individuals, often prioritizing the rights of individuals over the rights of the nation.
- **Proms equality and equality**: It promotes equality, often emphasizing the equality of individuals over the equality of the nation.
- **Priorizes freedom and freedom**: It prioritizes freedom, often emphasizing the freedom of individuals over the freedom of the nation.
- **Emphasizes inclusivity**: It emphasizes inclusivity, often prioritizing the inclusivity of individuals over the inclusivity of the nation.
