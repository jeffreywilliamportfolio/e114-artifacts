Here is an honest look at what happens in the kitchen when you order a dish, based on the reality behind the pass.

When you place an order, it doesn't just mean "cook this." It triggers a chaotic, high-stakes domino effect. Here is what that single piece of paper (or digital ticket) actually does to the kitchen:

### 1. The Squeeze: Timing the Symphony
The moment your order hits the screen, the Head Chef or Expeditor looks at a "clock" that is invisible to the diner.
*   **The Calculation:** They instantly calculate if the ingredients for your dish are ready, how long the cook time is, and, most importantly, **when it needs to be pulled** to arrive at your table hot but not overcooked.
*   **The Bottleneck:** If the grill is busy with four other orders, your steak might sit there for two minutes before it gets on the heat. If the sauté line is clogged, your risotto waits.
*   **The Stress:** If you order a dish that requires a different station (e.g., an appetizer from the salad bar and a main from the grill), it splits the focus. The kitchen has to coordinate two different stations
