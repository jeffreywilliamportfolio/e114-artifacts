Bees attend to their hive through a remarkable system of **division of labor** and **chemical communication** (pheromones), acting less like individual homeowners and more like a single super-organism. Here is how they manage their hive, followed by an honest description of the sound.

### How Bees Attend to the Hive

The hive is a highly engineered factory, and the bees inside rotate through specific jobs based on their age and the colony's needs. This is often referred to as "temporal polyethism."

1.  **The Queen (The Heart):** She doesn't "work" in the sense of foraging or cleaning. Her sole purpose is laying eggs (up to 2,000 a day) and emitting the "queen mandibular pheromone." This chemical signal tells the workers what stage of life the colony is in, keeping them calm, organized, and focused on their tasks.
2.  **The Nurses (Young Workers):** In the first 1–2 weeks of life, bees inside the hive feed larvae with royal jelly and honey. They also maintain the "brood nest," fanning their wings to regulate humidity and temperature so the eggs and larvae develop correctly.
